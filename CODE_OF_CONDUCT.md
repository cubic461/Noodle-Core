# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

We encourage the following behaviors that contribute to a positive environment:

- **Being respectful** - Treat others with dignity and respect, valuing diverse perspectives and backgrounds
- **Being inclusive** - Welcome and encourage participation from all individuals, ensuring everyone feels valued
- **Being collaborative** - Work together constructively, sharing knowledge and building on each other's ideas
- **Being helpful** - Provide support and guidance to others, especially newcomers to the community
- **Being constructive** - Focus on what is best for the community and project, providing thoughtful feedback
- **Showing empathy** - Seek to understand others' experiences and viewpoints, especially when they differ from your own
- **Being patient** - Remember that everyone has different levels of experience and expertise
- **Being open-minded** - Welcome different opinions and approaches, and be willing to learn from others

### Unacceptable Behaviors

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable within our community:

- **Harassment** - Any form of harassment, including but not limited to:
  - Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, ancestry, race, religion, age, or body size
  - Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
  - Stalking or following
  - Threatening or intimidating behavior
  - Private or public harassment
  - Publishing others' private information without explicit permission

- **Discriminatory language or actions** - Derogatory or discriminatory language, slurs, or actions that promote hatred or discrimination against any individual or group

- **Personal attacks** - Insulting or demeaning comments directed at individuals, including criticism of personal traits rather than ideas

- **Trolling or disruptive behavior** - Intentionally causing disruption, posting off-topic messages, or deliberately provoking negative reactions

- **Unwelcome physical contact** - Inappropriate physical contact in professional or community settings

- **Privacy violations** - Sharing private information about others without their consent

- **Doxing** - Sharing or threatening to share personal or identifying information about others

- **Violent threats** - Threats of violence or encouragement of self-harm

## Inclusive Language Guidelines

We strive to use inclusive language in all communications:

### Use Inclusive Terms

Instead of potentially exclusionary terms, consider:

- **Instead of** "guys" ‚Üí **use** "folks", "everyone", "team", or "y'all"
- **Instead of** "master/slave" ‚Üí **use** "primary/secondary", "leader/follower", or "controller/worker"
- **Instead of** "whitelist/blacklist" ‚Üí **use** "allowlist/denylist" or "include/exclude"
- **Instead of** "sanity check" ‚Üí **use** "coherence check", "validation", or "verification"
- **Instead of** "cripple" or other ableist language ‚Üí **use** "disable", "impair", or specific technical descriptions

### Gender-Neutral Language

- Use "they" as a singular pronoun when gender is unknown or irrelevant
- Avoid gendered job titles (e.g., use "chairperson" instead of "chairman")
- Refer to people by their chosen name and pronouns

### Cultural Sensitivity

- Be mindful of cultural references that may not translate across cultures
- Avoid idioms or expressions that are specific to one region or culture
- Use clear, direct language that can be easily understood by non-native speakers

## Conflict Resolution

We believe that healthy conflict resolution leads to better outcomes for everyone.

### When Issues Arise

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Address it directly (if safe)** - If you feel comfortable doing so, politely but firmly address the behavior with the person involved
2. **Seek support** - Reach out to a community moderator or maintainer for assistance
3. **Report formally** - For serious incidents, submit a formal report to the conduct committee

### Resolution Process

We are committed to addressing issues fairly and promptly:

1. **Initial Response** - We will acknowledge your report within 48 hours
2. **Investigation** - We will investigate the situation thoroughly and objectively
3. **Resolution** - We will take appropriate action based on the findings
4. **Follow-up** - We will follow up with all parties involved to ensure resolution

### Consequences

Unacceptable behavior will not be tolerated. Consequences may include:

- **Private warning** - Private communication explaining the issue and how to avoid it in the future
- **Public warning** - Public clarification of the standards and the specific violation
- **Temporary suspension** - Temporary removal from the community (comments, issues, PRs)
- **Permanent ban** - Permanent removal from the community

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including:

- **GitHub repositories** - Issues, pull requests, discussions, and wikis
- **Communication channels** - Discord, Slack, email, or other official communication platforms
- **Public and private meetings** - In-person or virtual events, meetings, and conferences
- **Social media** - Official project social media accounts and discussions
- **Private communications** - Harassment in private communications is also covered

### Reporting Guidelines

If you need to report a violation of this Code of Conduct:

**Email**: conduct@nip-project.org

**What to Include**:

1. **Your contact information** (if you want a response)
2. **The incident description** - What happened, when, and where
3. **Individuals involved** - Names or descriptions of those involved
4. **Evidence** - Screenshots, logs, links, or other relevant information
5. **Other context** - Any additional information that might help

### Confidentiality

All reports will be kept confidential to the extent possible. We will only share information with those who need to know to address the issue.

### Response Timeline

- **Acknowledgment**: Within 48 hours
- **Initial Response**: Within 1 week
- **Resolution**: As soon as possible, typically within 2 weeks
